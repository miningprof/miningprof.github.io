The Central Limit Theorem

A Proof via Characteristic Functions

1. Introduction

The Central Limit Theorem (CLT) is a fundamental result in probability theory. It states that the distribution of the sample mean of a sequence of sequence of independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) random variables converges to a standard normal distribution as the sample size approaches infinity, regardless of the underlying distribution of the variables.

2. Formal Statement

Theorem. Let \( X_1, X_2, \dots \) be a sequence of i.i.d. random variables with expected value \( \mathbb{E}[X_i] = \mu \) and finite variance \( \text{Var}(X_i) = \sigma^2 < \infty \). Then as \( n \to \infty \), the random variable $$ Z_n=\frac{\bar{X}_n - \mu}{\sigma / \sqrt{n}} $$ converges in distribution to a standard normal random variable \( Z \sim \mathcal{N}(0, 1) \).

3. The Proof Idea

The most common proof uses Characteristic Functions (which are essentially Fourier Transforms of probability distributions). The strategy is:

  1. Standardize the sum \( S_n = \sum X_i \).
  2. Find the characteristic function of the standardized sum.
  3. Take the limit as \( n \to \infty \) and show it equals the characteristic function of the Standard Normal distribution, which is \( e^{-t^2/2} \).

4. Step-by-Step Derivation

Let \( Y_i = \frac{X_i - \mu}{\sigma} \). Then \( Y_i \) has mean 0 and variance 1. The standardized variable \( Z_n \) can be rewritten as: $$ Z_n = \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}} \sum_{i=1}^n Y_i $$

Let \( \phi_Y(t) \) be the characteristic function of \( Y \). Since \( Y \) has mean 0 and variance 1, the Taylor expansion of \( \phi_Y(t) \) around \( t=0 \) is: $$ \phi_Y(t) = 1 - \frac{t^2}{2} + o(t^2) $$

Now consider the characteristic function of \( Z_n \): $$ \phi_{Z_n}(t) = \phi_{\sum Y_i}\left(\frac{t}{\sqrt{n}}\right) = \left[ \phi_Y\left(\frac{t}{\sqrt{n}}\right) \right]^n $$

Substituting the Taylor expansion: $$ \phi_{Z_n}(t) = \left[ 1 - \frac{(t/\sqrt{n})^2}{2} + o\left(\frac{t^2}{n}\right) \right]^n $$ $$ \phi_{Z_n}(t) = \left[ 1 - \frac{t^2}{2n} + o\left(\frac{t^2}{n}\right) \right]^n $$

Using the standard limit identity \( \lim_{n \to \infty} (1 - \frac{x}{n})^n = e^{-x} \), we get: $$ \lim_{n \to \infty} \phi_{Z_n}(t) = e^{-t^2/2} $$

5. Conclusion

The function \( e^{-t^2/2} \) is precisely the characteristic function of the standard normal distribution. By Lévy's Continuity Theorem, convergence of characteristic functions implies convergence in distribution.

Thus, we have proven that the normalized sum converges to a Standard Normal distribution.
