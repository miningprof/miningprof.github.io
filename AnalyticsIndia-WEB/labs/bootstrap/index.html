The Bootstrap

Pulling Yourself Up by Your Bootstraps

What if you only have ONE small sample of data? How can you know how accurate your average is?
The answer: Resample your own data.

The Cloning Machine

Pretend your single sample IS the entire population. Draw from it "with replacement" to create thousands of "fake" datasets.

Why it works

The variation inside your sample usually mirrors the variation in the real world. Bootstrapping lets us estimate standard error without fancy formulas.

Any Statistic

Works for Means, Medians, Correlation, or anything else. It's the "Swiss Army Knife" of modern statistics.

The Data Factory

We have one original sample of 10 numbers. Generate 1,000 resamples to see the range of likely values.
