Confidence Intervals

The Ring Toss Game

A 95% Confidence Interval doesn't mean "There is a 95% chance the mean is here."
It means "If you toss 100 rings, 95 will catch the pole."

The Hidden Truth

There is a SINGLE true population mean (the pole). It doesn't move. It isn't "probability". It is just a number.

The Ring (Interval)

Each experiment you do creates a random interval (the ring). Sometimes it catches the mean (Green). Sometimes it misses (Red).

Understanding 95%

It describes the reliability of your method, not the probability of the detailed result you just got.
