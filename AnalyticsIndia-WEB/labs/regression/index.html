The Line of Best Fit

We often want to predict one thing (y) based on another (x). But real data is messy. How do we draw the "best" line through the chaos?

Galton's Discovery

Francis Galton measured parents' heights vs. their children's heights. He found that tall parents have tall kids, but not quite as tall. This "regression to the mean" gave the field its name.

Financial Beta

In finance, "Beta" is just the slope of the regression line between a stock's return and the market's return. Steep slope = High Risk.

Machine Learning

Linear Regression is the "Hello World" of AI. Even complex neural networks are fundamentally trying to minimize error, just like this line.

What is "Least Squares"?

Why do we square the errors? Why not just add them up?

1. Squaring makes all errors positive (so -5 and +5 don't cancel out).
2. Squaring punishes outliers heavily. A mistake of 10 is 4 times worse than a mistake of 5.

The Objective Function

"Minimize the Sum of Squared Errors (SSE)."

SSE = Σ (yi - (mxi + b))²

The line you see automatically finds the specific m (slope) and b (intercept) that makes this number as small as possible.
