Central Limit Theorem

Why This Matters

The CLT is the reason we can predict the behavior of millions using just a handful of data points.

Election Polling

How do news networks predict a national election (150M voters) by calling just 2,000 people?

They rely on the CLT: the average of their random sample is mathematically guaranteed to be close to the true average.

Q.C. in Manufacturing

A factory makes 10,000 bolts a day. Are they strong enough? Breaking them all would be expensive.

Instead, they break 50. If the average strength of those 50 is good, the CLT says the whole batch is safe.

Insurance Risk

An insurer can't predict if you will crash your car. But they can predict perfectly how many of their 50,000 customers will crash.

Individual events are random; averages of large groups are predictable.

Origins of "The Bell Curve"

In 1809, Carl Friedrich Gauss was trying to model astronomical errors. He realized that small, random errors in measurement tended to cluster around a central value, forming a specific curve.

While De Moivre had seen it in coin flips earlier, Gauss showed that summing up many independent random errors naturally creates this "Normal Distribution." That's why it's often called the Gaussian Distribution.
