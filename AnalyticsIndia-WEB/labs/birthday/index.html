The Birthday Paradox

Coincidence? I think not.

How many people do you need in a room to have a 50% chance that two share a birthday?
Hint: It's way fewer than you think.

The Intuition Trap

we think "There are 365 days, so I need 183 people for a 50% chance."
But we aren't looking for a match with you. We are looking for any match between anyone.

Exponential Pairs

With 23 people, there are 253 possible pairs. That's 253 chances for a coincidence.

Hash Collisions

Computer security relies on this. "Collisions" (two passwords having the same hash) happen much faster than we expect, making systems vulnerable if the hash space is too small.
