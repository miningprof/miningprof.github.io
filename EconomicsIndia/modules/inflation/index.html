1. Why This Matters

Have you noticed that ₹100 doesn't buy as much as it used to? This is inflation. It acts like an invisible tax on your savings. If your income doesn't potential grow faster than inflation, you are actually getting poorer every year, even if your salary increases!

The Silent Thief

If inflation is 6% per year, your ₹1,00,000 savings will be worth only ₹50,000 in purchasing power after 12 years.

2. The Theory

Inflation is the rate at which the general level of prices for goods and services is rising.

Demand-Pull Inflation

"Too much money chasing too few goods." When people have more money to spend, but supply stays the same, prices go up.

Cost-Push Inflation

When the cost of raw materials (like oil) or wages goes up, companies raise prices to maintain profits.
